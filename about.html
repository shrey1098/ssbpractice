About Services Selection Board (SSB)

The Service Selection Board (SSB) is an organization of the Indian Armed Forces, which selects officers for the Indian Army, Indian Navy, and Indian Air Force. The SSB is responsible for the recruitment of officers into the Indian Armed Forces.

The selection process at SSB is highly scientific, time tested and proven technique. It aims at assessing a candidates personality traits in context of the requirements in the armed forces. The SSB recommends candidates who have the potential to carry out the duties of an armed forces officer after rigourous training at the Military Academy.

SSB uses three different techniques to assess and the re-assess the same personal qualities of a candidate at their conscious, sub-conscious and un-conscious levels. The techniques are:

  1. Interview Technique
  2. Psycological Technique
  3. Group Testing Technique
The three techniques are used independently by different assessors and one assessor has no knowledge about the other assessor's assessment. This provides a three dimensional view of a candidate. If some trait is not clear with one technique then it can easily be varified with another technique.

SSB is a 5 day process and is divided into two stages:

  1. Stage 1 - Screening (Day 1)
  2. Stage 2 (Day 2-5)

The day wise Schedule is as follows:

Day - 1 is devoted only for Screening tests. There are two types of screening tests:

It is an Intelligence and Reasoning test which includes verbal and non verbal reasoning ability questions. These are tested against time therefor it is important to practice them. Follow this link to practice

OIR tests are marked on 5 levels:

  • Level 1 - score 90+
  • Level 2 - score 70-90
  • Level 3 - score 60-70
  • Level 4 - score 51-60
  • Level 5 - score below 50
Ideally it is good to score Level 3 and above.

PPDT has three stages:

  1. All the candidates will be sitting a hall. A picture will be flashed on the screen for 30 seconds and the candidates are required to write a short story about that picture in 4 minutes. Along with the story certain details are to also be identified like: Number of Characters, their age gender and mood. Story should be in a positive light and built around a main character.
  2. Next the candidates will be divided in groups of 15 and each group will one by one be asked to sit infront of the three assessors. One by One each candidate will Narrate their written story and give the key details as asked.
  3. Next, after everyone has narrated the candidates will be asked to do a discussion, the purpose of the discussion is to arrive at a common story for the group.


After completion of Day-1 tests, results will be declared and the screened in candidates will be staying at the SSB for the rest of the 4 days.

Day 2 is devoted towards psycological Testing which are as follows:

60 Words will be flashed on the screen from 15 seconds each. In those 15 seconds you have to see the word and write a meaningful sentence using that word on the sheet provided.

12 picture will be flashed on the screen for 30 seconds each. In those 30 seconds you have to see the picture and write a story on the picture on the sheet provided with in 4 minutes. Threfore, 4 and a half minutes for each picture.

Story should be based on a main character and should be in a positive light with a meaninful theme. A story must have a 2-3 line introduction, 6-8 lines of actions and 2-3 lines of conclusion.

60 Situations will be written on a sheet of paper with space below each situation. Below every situation you have write dwon what you would do in that given situation. Remeber your responses must be logical, sensible and realistic. Time alloted in 30 minutes- try to attempt as many as possible.

You will be given a sheet of paper with 4 standard questions. You have to write down your answers in the space provided. The 4 questions are:

  1. What is the opinion of your parents about you?
  2. What is the opinion of your teachers/superiors about you?
  3. What is the opinion of your friends/colleagues about you?
  4. What do you think about yourself giving out your strong and weak points?
Self Description is the only test where you already know the questions, therefore prepare your answers before going for SSB.


Mostly the psych tests will be conducted in the morning hours, and thus the interviews may begin later that day.


After Psych tests interviews will begin and can happen on any day till day 4 depending on number of candidates and availibility of interviewing officers.

The Interview is conducted by 1 Officer for a candidate and is conducted in a very condusive atmosphere. The Candidate is first made comfortable by being general question like Introduction, then the officer will ask you questions depending on the answers you give.

Before the interview you will asked to fill out a Personal Information Questionnaire (PIQ) form, based on which some questions will be asked.


Day 3 and 4 are designated as GTO-1 and GTO-2 Days. In these two days a Group testing Officer conducts a series of 9 tests of different nature to assess the qualities of a candidate on ground.

For GTO the batch will be divided into smaller groups.

The Nine tests are as follows:

Group discussion is the first test in GTO tests. It is conducted outdoors. The group is given two topics with leads to discuss. Now it is important to pick a lead and stick to it during the entire course of discussion for a topic.

Listen to everyone points and contribute yours in a civilised and officer like manner. Remeber the objective of GD is to discuss a topic and not to convince others as why you are correct and other are wrong.

In GPE the entire group is given a situation along with a representation on Map. In this the entire group put in a hypothetical situtation where multiple problems have occurred and the group has limited resources to solve all the problems.

After the situation is briefed by the GTO- candidates get time to write down their solutions on a sheet of paper in a systematic manner. Next the group is asked to discuss and arrive at a common solution for all the problems.

After the discussion the GTO asks the group to select one person to narrate the final solution disussed by the group.

In Lecturette you will be given a card with three topics on it, you are required to select one topic and prepare a lecture on it for 3 minutes. The speaking time is also 3 minutes.

The topics are usually related to current affairs, social issues, politics, sports, etc. Good knowledge of current affairs is essential.


You may also have your interviews after GTO on day 3 and day 4.


Day 5 is designated for the Board Confrence, during which, all the three assessor will be present and discuss the candidate before deciding the final acceptability of the candidate. The Candidate is later called in and a final a final interview is done with the board to re-check anything if it is doubtful.


Finally a candidate is either recommended or not recommended for serivce. Recommended candidates futher go for Medical check ups in the next week.

Note: Preparation for SSB should start as soon as possible. One must not wait for the call letter. Practice is essential, regualr practice will make a candidate confident and will help him/her to perform better in the SSB. For practice follow this link.
